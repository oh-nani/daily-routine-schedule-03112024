On Days with 100% Energy (Full-Energy Day)

When you are feeling your best and fully energized, this is the schedule you will follow

Morning Routine

  1. 6:30 AM

    Wake up. Starting your day 30 minutes earlier will give you the time you need for a fulfilling morning routine without feeling rushed.

  2. 6:40 AM

    Begin with a brief mindfulness exercise or meditation to gently wake your mind and set a positive intention for the day (10 minutes).

  3. 6:50 AM

    Engage in a 30-minute workout. This can be a mix of cardio, strength training, yoga, or any physical activity that you enjoy and gets you moving (30 minutes).

  4. 7:20 AM

    Spend time with your dogs and water your plants. This period can also serve as a cool-down from your workout, getting some fresh air and sunlight (20 minutes).

  5. 7:40 AM

    Get ready for the day (shower, breakfast, etc.) (50 minutes).

  6. 8:30 AM

    Dedicate this time to working on a skill of your choice. This is a great way to engage your mind in learning and personal growth before starting your workday (30 minutes).

Workday

  1. 9:00 AM

    Start work. Focus on lighter, less demanding tasks if possible.

  2. Breaks (10:30 AM, 12:00 PM, 3:00 PM)

    Use your lunch and other breaks for restorative practices like deep breathing or a short, gentle walk if you're up for it.

Evening Routine

  1. 6:00 PM

    Work ends. You might unwind with a leisurely activity or another walk with your dogs to help transition from work mode (30 minutes).

  2. 6:30 PM

    Dinner and any chores or errands that need attention (1 hour).

  3. 7:30 PM

    This time can be used for leisure activities, additional skill development, or journaling to reflect on your day (1 hour).

  4. 8:30 PM

    Spend time talking with your partner. This is crucial for maintaining a strong bond and supporting each other's well-being (1.5 hours).

  5. 10:00 PM

    Start your bedtime routine, potentially incorporating a pre-sleep meditation to help ensure a restful night (30 minutes).

  6. 10:30 PM

    Lights out, aiming for your 9-10 hours of sleep.

Notes:

Flexibility Is Key: You might find that some mornings are more rushed than others, or you may need to adjust your workout length. Being flexible and forgiving with yourself is important as you establish this new routine.
Listening to Your Body: On days when you're not feeling up to a full workout, consider a lighter activity like stretching or a gentle yoga session to keep the habit without overexerting yourself.

Adjusting your morning to include a workout might mean you have to be more efficient with your time, but it can significantly enhance your overall well-being and energy levels.
