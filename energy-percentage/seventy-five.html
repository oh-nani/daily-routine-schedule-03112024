On Days with 75% Energy (Higher-Energy Day)

When you're feeling better than half but not fully energized, this schedule balances productivity with self-care.

Morning Routine

  1. 6:30 AM

    Wake up.

  2. 6:40 AM

    Engage in a more moderate workout than your most vigorous routine (20 minutes).

  3. 7:00 AM

    Mindfulness or meditation (10 minutes).

  4. 7:10 AM

    Time with dogs and plants, enjoying the morning light (20 minutes).

  5. 7:30 AM

    Get ready with more energy, maybe trying something new for breakfast or spending a bit more time on yourself (1 hour).

Workday

  1. 9:00 AM

    Start work. Focus on lighter, less demanding tasks if possible.

  2. Breaks (10:30 AM, 12:00 PM, 3:00 PM)

    Use your lunch and other breaks for restorative practices like deep breathing or a short, gentle walk if you're up for it.

Evening Routine

  1. 6:00 PM

    After work, choose a restful activity. This could be more time with your pets or listening to soothing music and also watering your plants.

  2. 6:30 PM

    Try to do some light workout. Maybe put on some music while walking or do a little yoga just to wind down.

  3. 7:00 PM

    Have a simple dinner, possibly something pre-prepared or easy to make.

  4. 8:00 PM

    Dedicate this time to working on a skill of your choice. This is a great way to engage your mind in learning and personal growth before starting your workday (30 minutes).

  5. 8:30 PM

    Talk with your partner, sharing that you're having a low-energy day, which can help in feeling supported.

  6. 10:00 PM

    Start winding down early for bed, skipping more demanding activities.

Notes:

Flexibility Is Key: You might find that some mornings are more rushed than others, or you may need to adjust your workout length. Being flexible and forgiving with yourself is important as you establish this new routine.
Listening to Your Body: On days when you're not feeling up to a full workout, consider a lighter activity like stretching or a gentle yoga session to keep the habit without overexerting yourself.

Adjusting your morning to include a workout might mean you have to be more efficient with your time, but it can significantly enhance your overall well-being and energy levels.
